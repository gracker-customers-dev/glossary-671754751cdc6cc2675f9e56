Tactical threat intelligence

Insights and information that guide operational security decisions and responses to immediate threats.

Description

Tactical threat intelligence refers to actionable information that provides security teams with the necessary context to respond to and mitigate immediate threats to an organization's infrastructure. This type of intelligence typically focuses on the methods, tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) used by cyber adversaries. By understanding these TTPs, security professionals can enhance their monitoring, detection, and response capabilities against ongoing attacks or vulnerabilities. Tactical intelligence often includes indicators of compromise (IOCs), such as specific IP addresses, domain names, file hashes, and patterns of malicious behavior that can be used to identify and block threats in real time. It is crucial for threat detection and response tools, as it enables organizations to prioritize their responses based on the severity and likelihood of potential threats, thus improving their overall security posture. Tactical threat intelligence is often sourced from various feeds, including vendor-specific sources, community sharing platforms, and internal data collection efforts.

Examples

  • Analysis of malware behavior that identifies specific file hashes used in an attack.
  • Identification of phishing email templates commonly used by threat actors.

Additional Information

  • Tactical threat intelligence can be integrated into Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) systems.
  • It is often derived from collaboration with threat intelligence sharing communities.
